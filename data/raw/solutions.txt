## Problem: Lower Back Pain During Exercise

### Causes:
- Weak core muscles unable to support the spine
- Tight hip flexors pulling on the lower back
- Poor posture during sitting and exercise
- Overloading the spine before core is strong enough

### Solutions:
- Strengthen core gradually: dead bugs, bird dogs, glute bridges, planks
- Stretch hip flexors daily: kneeling hip flexor stretch, pigeon pose
- Avoid heavy spine-loading: no heavy deadlifts or back squats until back is strong
- Replace: use Romanian deadlifts with light weight, goblet squats, box squats
- Always warm up the back: cat-cow, child's pose before any workout

### Exercises SAFE for back pain:
- Glute bridges (builds posterior chain, protects back)
- Bird dog (core stability without spinal compression)
- Dead bug (deep core activation)
- Resistance band rows (upper back, no spinal load)
- Side-lying leg raises (glutes, no back involvement)
- Swimming or walking (low impact)
- Wall squats
- Kegels: 

### Exercises to AVOID until stronger:
- Heavy barbell squats
- Conventional deadlifts with heavy weight
- Sit-ups and crunches (spinal flexion under load)
- Any movement that causes shooting pain

### Precautions:
- Stop immediately if pain radiates down the leg (nerve involvement)
- Sleep with a pillow between knees if side sleeper
- Avoid prolonged sitting — stand every 45 minutes

## Problem: Body Weakness / General Low Strength

### Causes:
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Insufficient protein intake
- Poor sleep
- Low muscle mass

### Solutions:
- Progressive overload: gradually increase weight/reps each week
- Compound movements: squats (modified), rows, push-ups, lunges
- Protein: target 1.8-2.3g per kg body weight
- Sleep: 7-9 hours is essential for muscle repair
- Eat in a caloric surplus (for weight gain)

### Exercises to build overall strength (beginner):
- Push-ups (progress to incline → standard → decline)
- Dumbbell rows
- Goblet squat
- Dumbbell shoulder press
- Resistance band pull-aparts
- Romanian deadlift with light dumbbells

## Problem: Low Stamina / Cardio Fitness

### Solutions:
- Start with 15-20 minute walks, 3x per week
- Progress to brisk walking, then jogging intervals
- Don't do cardio the same day as heavy strength (or do it after)
- Focus on strength first — muscle mass improves overall metabolism and stamina

## Problem: Weight Gain (Underweight / Hardgainer)

### Diet strategy:
- Eat in a surplus: 300-500 kcal above maintenance
- Prioritize protein: eggs, chicken, paneer, dal, milk, curd
- Add calorie-dense foods: peanut butter, nuts, whole milk, rice, roti, banana
- Eat 5-6 smaller meals if struggling to eat large portions
- Liquid calories help: milk-based smoothies, lassi with added peanut butter

### Sample high-calorie Indian foods:
- Milk + banana + peanut butter smoothie (~500 kcal, 20g protein)
- Paneer bhurji with 3 rotis (~600 kcal, 30g protein)
- Dal makhani + rice + curd (~550 kcal, 22g protein)

### Workout for weight gain:
- Focus on heavy compound lifts (progressive)
- Limit cardio to 1-2x per week (don't burn the surplus)
- Rest 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group
- Shoulder exercises: Shoulder press(with bricks), front raises, rear delt fly, side raises

## Problem: Tight Shoulders / Poor Posture

### Solutions:
- Daily chest stretches (doorframe stretch)
- Band pull-aparts daily
- Strengthen upper back: rows, face pulls
- Avoid excessive phone/screen hunching
- Add wall angels to morning routine

## Recovery Protocols

### After a hard workout:
- Protein within 30-60 minutes
- Hydrate well
- Light stretching or walk the next day (active recovery)

### When to rest vs push:
- Pain level 0-3: continue with modifications
- Pain level 4-6: reduce intensity by 50%, focus on mobility
- Pain level 7+: rest, treat the issue, consult a professional if persists

### Sleep:
- Most important recovery tool
- Aim for 7.5-9 hours
- Poor sleep = poor muscle growth, higher injury risk
